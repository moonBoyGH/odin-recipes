Oxtail Stew

Description

Oxtail stew is a hearty and flavorful dish that features tender, succulent meat from the tail of cattle. Slow-cooked to perfection, oxtail is renowned for its rich, gelatinous texture and deep, savory taste. This stew often combines oxtail pieces with an array of vegetables such as carrots, onions, and celery, along with aromatic herbs and spices like thyme, bay leaves, and garlic. The stew is typically simmered for an extended period, allowing the flavors to meld and the meat to become incredibly tender, resulting in a dish that is both comforting and deeply satisfying. Served steaming hot, oxtail stew is a beloved culinary delight, perfect for warming up on chilly days and a testament to the art of slow cooking.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds (1.3kg) oxtails with separated joints
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil, plus more for roasting the vegetables
  • 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 large carrot, chopped
  • 1 celery rib, chopped
  • 3 whole cloves of garlic, peel still on
  • 1 bay leaf
  • A pinch of thyme
  • 2 (475 ml) cups of stock (chicken or beef)
  • 2 carrots, cut into 1-inch segments, large pieces also cut lengthwise
  • 2 parsnips, cut into 1-inch segments, large pieces also cut lengthwise
  • 2 turnips or rutabagas, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • Parsley

Steps

  1. Preparation:

    Pat the oxtails dry with paper towels and season them generously with salt and pepper.

    Chop the vegetables - onion, garlic, carrots, and celery.


  2. Brown the Oxtails:

    Heat the cooking oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.

    Add the oxtails in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pot.

    Brown them on all sides.

    Remove and set aside.


  3. Sauté Vegetables:

    4. In the same pot, reduce the heat to medium.

    Add the chopped onion and garlic.

    Sauté until they're softened and fragrant.

    Add the carrots and celery, cooking for a few minutes until they begin to soften.


  4. Make the Stew:

    Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir well to coat.

    Cook for a minute to get rid of the raw flour taste.

    Pour in the beef broth and red wine, if using.

    Scrape the bottom of the pot to loosen any browned bits (this adds flavor).

    Add the bay leaves, thyme, and season with salt and pepper to taste.


  5. Simmer:

    Return the browned oxtails to the pot, along with any juices that have collected.

    Bring the mixture to a boil.

    Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the stew simmer gently.

    This could take 2.5 to 3 hours or more.

    Check occasionally, stirring and ensuring the oxtails are tender.


  6. Adjust Seasoning and Serve:

    Once the oxtails are tender and the stew has thickened, taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.

    Remove the bay leaves.

    Serve the oxtail stew hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired.

    It's commonly served over rice, mashed potatoes, or with crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce.
